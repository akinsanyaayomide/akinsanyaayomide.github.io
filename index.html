About Me

I am a fifth-year Computer Science Ph.D. candidate specializing in the security and privacy of machine learning and AI systems. My research spans adversarial machine learning, federated learning, privacy-preserving techniques, and graph representation learning. I have gained hands-on industry experience through research internships at Microsoft, and LinkedIn, where I worked on applying AI to security and trust & safety challenges. I hold a Master's degree in Computer Science from the Stevens Institute of Technology, where I built a strong foundation in machine learning, deep learning, natural language processing, computer vision, and privacy-enhancing technologies. Driven by a passion for advancing AI security and privacy, I aim to bridge cutting-edge research with real-world impact. My work has been published in leading security venues such as NDSS 2024, MICCAI 2025 and ASIACCS 2026.
